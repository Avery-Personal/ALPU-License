1. PURPOSE
---

This document establishes the governance structure for the
ALPU (Asuka License for Protective Use) project, including
its variants (ALEPU, ALLPU), updates, and official releases.

It ensures consistent decision-making, version control, and
authority over the ALPU license and derivative works.

All references to ALPU v1.2 within this document are
authoritative for governance matters.

2. MAINTAINERS
---

2.1 Role
  - Maintainers are individuals or entities authorized by
    Asuka Org to approve changes, updates, or new license
    variants.

2.2 Responsibilities
  - Approve or reject proposed modifications to ALPU text
    or specifications.
  - Review derivative licenses (ALEPU, ALLPU) for compliance.
  - Maintain versioning and changelog records.
  - Respond to legal or community inquiries regarding ALPU.

2.3 Appointment
  - Maintainers are appointed by Asuka Org.
  - Term limits are indefinite unless revoked by Asuka Org.

3. DECISION-MAKING
---

3.1 Change Proposals
  - All proposals must be submitted in writing (email, PR,
    or official communication) to maintainers.
  - Each proposal must include:
      * Purpose of change
      * Text revisions
      * Legal implications
      * Versioning recommendation

3.2 Voting
  - Maintainers approve or reject proposals by consensus.
  - In cases where consensus cannot be reached, the
    Asuka Org Legal Officer has final authority.

3.3 Versioning
  - Semantic versioning is used: MAJOR.MINOR
      * MAJOR: breaking changes to permissions, restrictions,
        or definitions
      * MINOR: clarifications, formatting, or editorial changes

4. RELEASE POLICY
---

4.1 Official Releases
  - Only versions approved by maintainers are considered
    official ALPU releases.
  - Each release must include:
      * LICENSE_ALPU.txt
      * SPEC.md
      * CHANGELOG.md
      * PROJECT_NOTICE.md

4.2 Variant Releases
  - ALEPU, ALLPU, and other variants may only be released
    after review for compliance with the ALPU standard.
  - Variants must reference the base ALPU version they
    derive from.

4.3 Publication
  - All releases are published on the official ALPU GitHub
    repository or an approved official channel.

5. COMPLIANCE
---

5.1 Enforcement
  - Compliance with governance ensures consistency and
    legal reliability.
  - Unauthorized forks claiming to be “official ALPU” are
    invalid.

5.2 Audit
  - Maintainers may periodically review derivative licenses,
    references, or uses to ensure ALPU integrity.

5.3 Dispute Resolution
  - Any conflicts regarding governance, interpretation, or
    enforcement are escalated to the Asuka Org Legal Officer.

6. DOCUMENT MAINTENANCE
---

6.1 Updates
  - GOVERNANCE.md is maintained in parallel with ALPU
    LICENSE and SPEC.md updates.
  - Minor formatting changes do not require approval.
  - Substantive governance changes require formal
    consensus.

6.2 Archival
  - All previous versions of GOVERNANCE.md must be
    archived with timestamped records in the repository.
