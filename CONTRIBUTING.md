1. PURPOSE
---

This document outlines the procedures and rules for
submitting contributions, proposals, or derivative license
requests to the ALPU project and its variants (ALEPU, ALLPU).

All contributions are subject to the governance rules
defined in GOVERNANCE.md and the legal authority of Asuka Org.

2. WHO CAN CONTRIBUTE
---

2.1 Eligibility
  - Any individual or entity may submit proposals, requests,
    or feedback.
  - Submission does NOT automatically grant rights to modify
    the license or produce derivative works.

2.2 Rights
  - Contributors retain copyright on their submissions unless
    explicitly transferred.
  - By submitting, contributors grant Asuka Org the right
    to use, adapt, or incorporate their work into ALPU,
    ALEPU, or ALLPU.

3. SUBMISSION TYPES
---

3.1 License Text Proposals
  - Suggested changes to LICENSE_ALPU.txt, SPEC.md, or
    variant licenses.
  - Must include:
      * Current text
      * Proposed text
      * Justification and legal reasoning
      * Recommended version bump

3.2 Governance Proposals
  - Suggestions for updates to GOVERNANCE.md.
  - Must include:
      * Proposal description
      * Expected impact
      * Reference to affected sections

3.3 Issue Reporting
  - Any errors, typos, or inconsistencies in ALPU documentation
    or license files.
  - Include:
      * File and section number
      * Description of issue
      * Suggested fix (optional)

4. SUBMISSION PROCESS
---

4.1 Method
  - Submissions should be made via:
      * Pull request (PR) on the official ALPU repository
      * Email or authenticated message to maintainers
  - Include all required information and references.

4.2 Review
  - Maintainers will review each submission according to
    GOVERNANCE.md.
  - Proposals may be:
      * Accepted
      * Accepted with modifications
      * Rejected (with reasoning)

4.3 Communication
  - Maintainers may request clarification or revision.
  - Contributors are encouraged to respond within a
    reasonable timeframe.

5. DERIVATIVE LICENSE REQUESTS
---

5.1 Eligibility
  - Requests to create ALEPU, ALLPU, or other derivative
    licenses must be submitted formally.
  - Must include:
      * Base ALPU version
      * Proposed variant rules
      * Intended use case
      * Compliance strategy

5.2 Approval
  - Maintainers review and approve according to
    GOVERNANCE.md.
  - Approval is granted in writing and recorded in the
    repository.

6. LEGAL AND COMPLIANCE NOTES
---

6.1 No Implicit Rights
  - Submission does NOT grant automatic rights to modify
    ALPU or produce derivative works.

6.2 Licensing of Submissions
  - All contributions are licensed to Asuka Org under ALPU
    v1.2 for incorporation purposes.

6.3 Dispute Resolution
  - Any conflicts regarding submissions or approvals are
    resolved by the Asuka Org Legal Officer.

7. OFFICIAL NOTATION
---

All submitted files or communications should include:

  ALPU License v1.2 — © 2025 Asuka Org  
  Refer to LICENSE_ALPU.txt, SPEC.md, and GOVERNANCE.md for rules
  See CONTRIBUTING.md for submission guidelines
